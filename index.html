Level 2 · Science
Humans Come from Stars
▾
🔗 Open Original Article

Stars are born inside very cold clouds of gas and dust called molecular clouds. These clouds are very big and heavy. Small parts inside the clouds come together and grow bigger.

When they get heavy enough, they fall in on themselves and form baby stars called protostars. After some time, the inside gets hot, and a process called fusion starts. Fusion changes hydrogen into helium and gives the star energy. When a star uses all its hydrogen, it will become a white dwarf or a supernova. The star's materials help to make new stars in the future.

Old stars made the things in our bodies, like carbon and oxygen. These stars lived a long time before the Earth, the Sun, or our galaxy. When the stars exploded, they sent their elements across space. Later, these materials came together to make planets, stars, and people. Carl Sagan, a famous scientist, said that the universe is inside us and that we are made of star stuff. This means we are all connected to the stars in a very special way.

What Dopamine Does to Our Brain
▾
Level 2 ↗ Source

People often get hooked on their phones and devices even when they know it is bad for them.

This happens because of a chemical in our brain called dopamine. Dopamine makes us feel pleasure, motivation, and reward. When we scroll through social media, our brain releases dopamine, which makes us want to do it more. But if we keep doing this, our brain gets used to it and starts releasing less dopamine. This can cause problems like not being able to focus on other things.

The more we use our devices, the more we need new things to feel the same pleasure, which can lead to a feeling of stress. This makes it harder to enjoy real-life activities and may cause anxiety.
